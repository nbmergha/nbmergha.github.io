COVID-19 Deeply Affected New York in 2020



The first reported COVID-19 case in the United States was in January 2020, and it put the entire country in a state of emergency up until May 11, 2023 when President Joe Biden declared the COVID-19 public health emergency is over.


As one of the most populated areas in the United States, New York was deeply affected by the pandemic. The first reported case was on March 1, 2020. After that, a total of 990,711 reported cases occured that year.

In Figure 1, you can see the breakdown of reported cases by month.





In 2020, over 25 million COVID-19 vaccines were distributed in the state of New York. These vaccines were used by those who suspected they may have COVID-19 to test whether or not they did have the disease. Those that tested positive were required by law to quarantine. However, part of the reason COVID-19 spread so quickly is because people often disobeyed quarantine orders.
